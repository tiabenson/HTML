Grown-ish

My favorite television series is Grown-ish.  This is a spin-off from the popoular series Black-ish, which follows the Johnson family finding their ethnic identity while living in an upper-class neighborhood. Grown-ish follows the life of the eldest daughter in the family, Zoey Johnson, as she attends college.  She soon realizes that everything will not go as smoothly as she planned.

I love this series because the show addresses the obstacles that many students face in college.  As a college junior, I can say that the show's content is pretty accurate. Another reason I love this series is due to the diverse backgrounds of the characters.  There is Jewish, African-American, LGBT, and Indian representation.  Grown-ish has also become popular in terms of fashion, so it's nice to watch each episode to see the latest trends.

Three other main characters of Grown-ish are:

  • Nomi Segal
  • Skylar Forster
  • Vivek Shah
Character Background Picture
Nomi Segal A Jewish, sexually fluid feminist.  Her uncle is the dean of the university and doesn't know about her sexuality.  She isn't afraid to question society's rules.
Skylar Forster Skylar is a twin sister to Jazz Forster, who also attends California University. She is involved in track and field.  Due to her upbringings, she feels pressure from her community to become successful.
Vivek Shah An Indian American whose parents want him to become an engineer.  He's a drug dealer on campus to distract others from seeing him as an Indian sterotype.  His father is a taxi driver, and he doesn't want to be known as the "taxi driver's son."
